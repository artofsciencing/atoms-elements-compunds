What is an Atom?
An atom is the smallest particle of an element that still has the chemical properties of that element. Everything around you — air, water, metal — is made of atoms!

Every atom has a nucleus (containing protons ➕ and neutrons ⚪) surrounded by electrons ➖ orbiting in shells.
⚡ Protons: positive charge
⚪ Neutrons: no charge
🔵 Electrons: negative charge
🔶
Elements
An element is a pure substance made of only one type of atom. There are over 100 elements — each has a unique chemical symbol of 1 or 2 letters.

Elements are either metals (e.g. iron, gold, sodium) or non-metals (e.g. oxygen, carbon, nitrogen).
Examples: H (Hydrogen) · O (Oxygen) · Fe (Iron) · Na (Sodium) · Au (Gold)
🔗
Molecules
A molecule is formed when two or more atoms bond together. Molecules can be made from atoms of the same element or different elements.

The number of atoms is shown in the chemical formula as a subscript number.
H₂ — 2 hydrogen atoms
O₂ — 2 oxygen atoms
H₂O — 2 hydrogen + 1 oxygen
🧬
Compounds
A compound is a substance made from two or more different elements chemically combined in fixed ratios. Its properties are completely different from the elements it contains!

Compounds can only be separated by chemical reactions.
NaCl — Sodium + Chlorine → Table salt
H₂O — Hydrogen + Oxygen → Water
CO₂ — Carbon + Oxygen → Carbon dioxide
